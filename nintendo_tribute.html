Nintendo Entertainment System

  • Originally released in Japan in 1983
  • Debuted in the US in 1985
  • Bundled with Super Mario Bros., one of the best-selling video games of all time
  • Helped revitalize and set a standard business model for the US video gaming industry
Super Nintendo Entertainment System

  • Originally released in Japan in 1990
  • Debuted in the US in 1991
  • Introduced advanced graphics and sound capabilities unparalleled by any other home console
  • The ability to add enhancement chips to cartridge circuit boards offered 3D effects, and a competitive edge
Nintendo 64

  • Launched in 1996
  • Featured 3D polygon model rendering capabilities
  • Rumble Pack for the Y Controller, and built in multi-player functionality became standardized features for home consoles
  • It's sister console, Nintendo 64DD was only released in Japan in 1999 due to commercial failure
GameCube

  • Released in North America and Japan in 2001
  • Competed with Sony's Playstation 2, Microsoft Xbox, Sega's Dreamcast
  • First Nintendo console to use optical disc
  • Supports internet gaming and Game Boy Advance connectivity
Wii

  • Released worldwide in 2006
  • Nintendo's best selling home video game console
  • Features include WiiConnect24, and Virtual Console
  • First home video game console to use motion controls
Wii U

  • Released worldwide in 2012
  • Nintendo's first console to support high-definition graphics
  • Primary controller is the Wii U GamePad, which has an embedded touchscreen that can be utilized instead of a television
  • The Wii U is backward compatible with Wii software and accessories
